01

Data-Driven Series Development

Scaling original content for OTT growth +
Challenge

The platform set out to grow original content from 5% to 20% of the monthly library — which meant increasing both the volume and the strategic quality of series concepts to justify the investment.

What I did
  • Built a market-intelligence foundation: a unified dashboard synthesizing 15+ data sources — consumption trends, competitor gaps, social listening, search patterns — to find white-space opportunities.
  • Developed a concept-to-projection pipeline: each opportunity became a full concept with a creative brief, world-building guidelines, and a data-backed business case with ROI and audience projections.
  • Streamlined the path to launch: documented cross-team workflows that synced marketing, PR, and platform teams from day one.
Outcome
  • A 30% executive greenlight rate, consistently filling the development slate with high-potential projects.
  • 20+ strategic licensing deals closed using the same data frameworks.
  • Shorter, more predictable launch cycles and higher annual output.
Skills: Content Strategy · Data Analysis · Business Case Development · Workflow Optimization · Cross-functional Leadership
02

Launch Protocol Overhaul

From reactive firefighting to proactive alignment +
Challenge

Series often arrived with finished assets just before launch, leaving no time for review — so creative or quality issues surfaced only after premiere, forcing rushed, public-facing fixes.

What I did
  • Redefined the production timeline, building dedicated review windows to protect creative time.
  • Created a Concept Kick-Off meeting that brings executives, producers, designers, and post-production together around one creative brief and vision.
  • Instituted a Launch Approval gate two weeks before premiere — a structured showcase of final assets, not a surprise reveal.
  • Built in a correction buffer, turning panic fixes into planned, high-quality iterations.
Outcome
  • Sharply reduced post-launch issues, protecting brand quality from day one.
  • Clearer creative direction from the outset — less rework and better team morale.
  • Executives integrated as creative partners, leading to faster, more confident sign-offs.

Data Analysis · Power BI

Netflix Catalog Analysis

An end-to-end BI project on Netflix's catalog using public Kaggle datasets (5,800+ titles, 77K+ credits). I cleaned and modeled the data in Power Query — splitting multivalue columns, correcting the score scale, handling nulls, and building table relationships — then designed a three-page interactive dashboard with a custom theme. Key findings: series drive disproportionate engagement versus films, launches concentrate in Q4, and romance and comedy lead average scores — closing with strategic recommendations on format, launch windows, and local-market content.
